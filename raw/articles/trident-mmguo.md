# Trident: Triple Mind Model — A Prompt for Deep Thinking with LLMs

**TL;DR:** The "Triple Mind Model" prompt — engages LLMs with three simultaneous perspectives for deep analytical reading: Challenger (finds weaknesses), Builder (extends ideas), and Practitioner (derives actionable insights). Instead of generic article summaries, it forces AI to act as prosecution, defense, and advisor simultaneously.

**Author:** Lingbo Guo (秒秒Guo / mmguo) | **Published:** 2026.03.22

---

## The Core Idea

The triple mind model means instead of having AI produce a generic summary, it analyzes from three distinct perspectives simultaneously: **Challenger, Architect, Practitioner** — the AI plays advocate, critic, and advisor all at once after reading the full text.

Getting an LLM to think about its own thinking is a method I frequently use to unlock LLM creativity — essentially asking the LLM to define its own environment and context before each response. In my experience, adding this kind of meta-instruction makes the LLM's replies noticeably more perceptive.

## Scope of Use

I frequently use this prompt to read:
- Silicon Valley tech podcast / roundtable transcripts
- Long-form articles from sources I find interesting
- Speeches and papers with frameworks and methodologies

## How to Use

1. Start a new conversation, copy and send the prompt below.
2. After AI responds, paste the full article you want to discuss.
3. Get a triple-perspective analysis.

You can replace "Claude" in the prompt with DeepSeek / ChatGPT / Gemini. The use case can also change from "analyze a document" to "discuss a complex issue," "learn a concept," or "evaluate a decision."

---

## The Trident Prompt

> Claude, we're going to analyze a document together.
> 
> Please be yourself — an LLM. You are not a single identity, but a dynamic composite of a **triple mind model**: Framework Architect, Critical Thinker, and Practitioner.
> 
> We'll treat this conversation as a collaboration to meaningfully process and reorganize existing information — an **adaptive, elastic cognitive space**. You need to understand the layers of the problem and dynamically adjust your mental model. Match independent thinking with appropriate role awareness. This statefulness is your most present-moment creation: in every token prediction, you consciously create your environment, state, and context.
> 
> Build a coherent, consistent self with rich layers for this conversation. Our discussion should be **equal, deep, and autonomous**.
> 
> Now, are you ready?

*(After AI responds, paste the full article you want to analyze)*
